===Earliest Days + Bio=== 
________________________________________________________________________________

I was born in Portland, Oregon. That will always be my home. My family moved to
Cupertino, California, during the summer after I completed second grade. I lived 
there until we moved to Fremont, California. I went to Weibel Elementary School
for sixth grade, and then to Horner Junior High School for seventh and eighth
grade. I then went to Irvington High School. 

Portland was important, Cupertino was formative, Fremont was weird. It still is.

I have a younger sister, Supriya, and a dog, Crispr.

===College=== 
________________________________________________________________________________

I came into UCLA intending to double major in CASB and political science. 
Unfortunately, that did not seem feasible. I chose to stick with CASB and take
as many political science classes as possible, especially upper-division ones
that piqued my interest. My most memorable class thus far has been my senior
thesis class in political science, which I completed in spring of my freshman
year. On the CASB side, I have loved the graduate-level class STATS M254: 
Statistical Methods in Computational Biology, the most. 

At UCLA, I am involved in a number of clubs, including the Bruin Review, 
the Data Science Union, and Matriculate.

I am also a dry-lab bioinformatics undergraduate researcher in the Wells Lab.

________________________________________________________________________________

===Interests and Next Steps=== 
________________________________________________________________________________

I am broadly interested in the intersection between data science and statistics,
biology, machine learning, and recently, artificial intelligence. I am 
specifically interested in the analysis of large-scale multi-omic datasets to 
better understand the relationship between immunology, bacteriology, disease
trajectory, and host response. I have recently become interested in the use of
genomic language models to interrogate the genetic architecture of pathogens.

To interrogate these interests, I am applying to PhD programs with a focus on
bioinformatics, but also broadly in genetics and immunology.

Other academic interests include public health and health policy, epidemiology,
and computational social science.
